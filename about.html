About Me

My name is Charles, I'm 32 and I've had prior experience with some minor web coding (HTML and CSS). Throughout my twenties, I co-hosted and ran a few fan based art and writing fests online which gave me tons of experience in various areas including: sending out emails, designing advertising and web design, pairing up partners, dealing with backend data, releasing news and event submissions, moderating discord chats, and much more.

For fun, my favorite hobbies are to draw, paint, read, write, edit, code, game, crochet, knit, and above all of that, I love to learn. The core theme or reason for my enjoyment is creation. I often jump back and forth on what my current all-time focus is when releasing everyday stress, but I always come back around to them after losing interest for a while.

I started learning at Correlation One for Software Development mid-July. My current place of employment offered a full free ride while I could learn some valuable skills. I thought why not give it a go. So now here I am, enjoying challenging myself with new ideas, resources, tools, and skills. Learning new concepts can be difficult at times, but with a drive to understand and focused practice it's easy to pick up, while being super rewarding.

So far, I have learned more advanced HTML and CSS with tool kits such as Bootstrap and SASS. I've been introduced and have started using VS Code to work on my projects and Node.js to help compile SASS files. Just recently, we've been introduced to gitHub and the terminal for uploading our site and projects. I'm excited to see how this will help us in the future.

In the upcoming weeks we will get into working with WordPress (which I have a little familarity with), Javascript, and some Frontend-Frameworks. I have read a bit of web based Javascript, and I've had an animation class in high school that worked with some basic java, so I won't be in completely new territory, however I do know that javascript is a vast language and it's definitely going to be a rollercoaster of frustration, comprehension, and rewarding feelings. Even so, I'm excited to learn new things and see where this new knowledge can lead me.
